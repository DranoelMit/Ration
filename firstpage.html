About Me

As an Electrical Engineering student at Stevens Institute of Technology, I am enthusiastic about using technology for social good and stretching our imagination to comprehend and make use of the discoveries in the universe. I have interests in renewable energy, off- line electric work, website development and nanotechnology.

I completed the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program in 2016, where I worked on web development and built and programmed robots and circuits. I also gained exposure to the professional side of the technology industry. I was the instructor for a Girls Who Code Club, where I taught refugee girls in the community how to code and use circuits. In addition, I monitored academic and team progress throughout the program. I am currently teaching an entry level coding class with the non- profit HOPES, where I teach the basic fundamentals of programming and assist the students on the completion of long- term projects.
